Jean-Michel Basquiat functioned less as a painter and more as a system for revealing how thought behaves before it becomes organized. His work operates in that unstable zone where information, instinct, and cultural memory intersect without agreeing on a hierarchy. Instead of offering clarity, he constructed visual frameworks that behave like unfinished arguments, structures that appear intentional yet resist any final interpretation.
